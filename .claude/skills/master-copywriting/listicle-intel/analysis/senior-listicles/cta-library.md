# Senior Listicles - CTA Library

These CTAs are designed for the senior demographic - lower friction, trust-building, dignity-preserving.

## CTA Formatting for Seniors

### Visual Requirements
- Large button (minimum 44px height for touch)
- High contrast (dark text on light, or white on dark)
- Generous padding
- Clear, readable font (16px+ for subtext)

### Button Text Rules
- 2-5 words maximum
- Active voice ("See My Options" not "Options Available")
- First person when possible ("My" not "Your")
- Avoid jargon

### Subtext Rules
- One line, 8-12 words max
- Address top objection
- Include time/effort estimate
- Use trust signals

## A/B Testing Notes

### What to Test
1. Button color (blue vs green often win)
2. "My" vs "Your" language
3. With/without subtext
4. With/without phone number
5. Trust signal placement

### Senior-Specific Findings
- Phone numbers increase trust significantly
- "No credit check" outperforms other subtexts for financial
- Larger buttons = higher click rates
- Multiple CTA options (button + phone) beat single CTA

## Anti-Patterns

### CTAs That Fail with Seniors

**Too Aggressive:**
- "Buy Now" (pressure)
- "Don't Miss Out!" (FOMO overload)
- "Limited Time!" (false urgency)

**Too Vague:**
- "Learn More" (learn what?)
- "Get Started" (started with what?)
- "Click Here" (outdated, unhelpful)

**Too Complex:**
- "Submit Your Application for Pre-Qualification Review"
- "Begin the Enrollment and Verification Process"

**Too Casual:**
- "Let's Do This!"
- "Heck Yeah!"
- "YOLO Savings!"
