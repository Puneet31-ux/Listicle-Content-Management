# Grammy Notes: Senior Listicle User Research

**Source:** Real user testing with senior audience member ("Grammy")
**Context:** Testing senior discount listicle at https://2025.topdiscountsforseniors.com/default/
**Date:** Captured during user feedback session

---

## Visual Preferences

### Model/Imagery Guidelines
- ✅ **Good:** Model should look "befuddled" or "overwhelmed"
  - Conveys: "I could use help" or feeling overwhelmed by options
  - Relatable, approachable, seeking guidance

- ❌ **Bad:** Model looks "disgruntled" or "ornery"
  - Looks like "there's something wrong with her"
  - Off-putting, unapproachable tone

- ✅ **Good:** Pleasant, relatable senior who appears to need assistance
- ❌ **Bad:** Stock corporate senior, overly happy/fake

**Application:** Use in `visual-guidelines/seniors-imagery.md` for all senior-focused offers

---

## Copy & CTA Preferences

### CTA Language
- ✅ **"Learn More"** - Preferred, lower pressure
  - User: "Don't want to convert now but want to learn more"
  - Information-seeking, non-committal

- ❌ **"Get Ready to Transform"** - Too pushy
- ❌ **"Transform Your Equity"** - Confusing, too aggressive

### Tone Preferences
- Value-focused: "Good value" resonates strongly
- Specific examples work: Chick-fil-A discount mentioned positively
- Clear, straightforward language over marketing jargon

## Real Pain Points & Financial Situations

### Investment Experiences
- **Lost money on investments:** Lost $12K with investment advisor
- **Current strategy:** High-yield money market (was 5.26%, now down to 3.8%)
- **Need:** MONTHLY interest income (not just principal growth)
  - Example: Getting interest check every month is important
  - Current: Less than $400/month at 3.8% on Vanguard money market

### Tax & Financial Mistakes
- **Rollover confusion:** Thought was rolling into same product, but it was different
  - Result: Government taxed 5% unexpectedly
- **Financial advisor barriers:** Most require $500K+ minimum
  - "Most people don't have the funds or money to hire financial advisor"

### Key Insight
> "PEOPLE WANT TO KNOW HOW TO KEEP MORE MONEY IN THEIR OWN POCKET!!"

**Application:** Emphasize monthly income, not just APY rates. Highlight tax implications and accessibility (no minimums).

## Specific Offer Interests & Use Cases

### High-Yield Savings
- **Varo Bank:** Friend uses it, pays "almost 5% interest"
- **Trust signal:** FDIC-backed (explicitly mentioned as important)
- **Copy angle:** Emphasize monthly income, not annual percentages
  - "Get $X per month" > "5% APY"

### Solar Energy Savings
**Personal Experience (Solar Farm Participation):**
- **Provider:** Arcadia
- **Model:** Part of community solar farm (not roof installation)
- **Cost:** Didn't cost anything upfront
- **Savings:** 15-20% per month
- **Results:**
  - August: Paid only $15 for electric
  - September: Paid nothing + $58 credit!
  - "Friend gets a credit!"

**Friend's Experience (Roof Installation):**
- Installed 10 years ago
- "Loves it! Cost a lot less for energy"
- Some months get a credit back

**Copy angles:**
- Community solar farm as alternative to roof installation (no upfront cost)
- Specific monthly savings ($15 total electric bill vs typical $100+)
- Credit opportunities (some months you GET paid)

### Final Expense / Long-Term Care
- **Final expense:** "This is a big thing!" - Prepaid funeral planning
- **Long-term care insurance:** Friend has "Cadillac of long-term health plans"
  - Purchased 15 years ago
  - "Getting EVERYTHING PAID FOR" in assisted living facility
  - Facility is expensive but plan covers it

**Copy angle:** Emphasize peace of mind, paid vs paid-ahead-of-time contrast

### Debt Consolidation
- **Current situation:** "Can't believe how many people are in debt... in the trillions"
- **Messaging opportunity:**
  - "Average 27% [credit card rate] vs down to fixed rate of maybe 10-15%"
  - Show specific rate reduction, not just "lower rates"

### Car Insurance, Health, Life Insurance
- Interest in finding savings across all these categories
- "Where to go to FIND THE INFO ON TAX SAVINGS ETC — REAL HELPFUL INFO"

## Trust Signals That Matter

### Financial Offers
1. **FDIC-backed** (explicitly mentioned for Varo Bank)
2. **Real examples from friends/neighbors** (solar friend, LTC insurance friend, Linda)
3. **Specific numbers/proof** ($58 credit, $15 electric bill, 5.26% → 3.8%)
4. **Longevity proof** (solar installed 10 years ago, LTC plan from 15 years ago)

### Credibility Patterns
- Personal testimonials > Corporate marketing
- Friend success stories > Celebrity endorsements
- Specific financial outcomes > Vague promises

## Category-Specific Insights

### Financial (High-Yield Savings, Debt Relief)
- **Pain:** Interest rate drops (5.26% → 3.8% hurts monthly income)
- **Fear:** Investment losses ($12K loss still remembered)
- **Barrier:** Can't afford financial advisors ($500K+ minimums)
- **Need:** Monthly income reliability
- **Trust:** FDIC-backed, government-insured

**Copy priorities:**
1. Monthly income emphasis (not APY)
2. FDIC/government backing
3. Specific rate reduction (27% → 10-15%)
4. No minimums, accessible

### Home Services (Solar)
- **Pain:** High electric bills (typical $100+)
- **Fear:** Upfront cost, roof damage concerns
- **Opportunity:** Community solar (no installation, no upfront cost)
- **Trust:** Friend testimonials, 10+ year track record
- **Value:** Specific monthly savings + occasional credits

**Copy priorities:**
1. No upfront cost (community solar farm model)
2. Specific monthly savings ($15 vs $100+ bill)
3. Credit opportunities (some months negative bill)
4. No roof installation needed (alternative positioning)

### Insurance (Final Expense, Long-Term Care)
- **Pain:** Facility costs ("expensive")
- **Fear:** Running out of money in old age
- **Value:** "Cadillac plan" gets everything paid for
- **Trust:** 15-year track record, friend success story

**Copy priorities:**
1. Paid vs paid-ahead contrast
2. Coverage comprehensiveness ("everything paid for")
3. Long-term value (buy now, use later)
4. Facility quality maintained despite costs
